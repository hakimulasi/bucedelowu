Who Are Her Parents and What Happened at University of Kentucky?

August 2024 · 2 minute read

Sophia Rosing, a student at the University of Kentucky, made headlines after being recorded hurling racial insults at fellow student Kylah Spring while intoxicated. Rosing allegedly used the n-word over 200 times while assaulting Spring, who was working at the front desk of the Boyd Hall dorm building. The incident led to several charges, including disorderly conduct, assault, assault on a police officer, and alcohol intoxication in a public place.

Who Are Sophia Rosing’s Parents?

Sophia Rosing is the youngest child of Paul Donald Rosing Jr. and Jill Algie Rosing. Her father works for Messer Construction Co. as a Technology Executive, and she grew up in their three-bedroom house with her family.

Sophia’s Court Hearing

During her court hearing, Sophia Rosing pleaded not guilty to all charges and was released from the detention center after her parents posted a $10,000 bond. Her release terms prohibit her from contacting Kylah Spring, returning to Boyd Hall, and drinking alcohol.

Sophia’s Parents Attend Court Hearing in Support

Sophia Rosing’s parents attended her court hearing in support of her following the alleged incident involving Kylah Spring. The hearing took place on Monday, November 7, via a video link. Sophia pleaded not guilty to charges of intoxication in a public place, third-degree assault on a police officer, fourth-degree assault, and second-degree disorderly conduct.

The University of Kentucky President’s Statement

Kentucky University President Eli Capilouto condemned racism and praised Spring for how she dealt with the alleged incident. In an official statement posted online, Capilouto said, “From my view of a video of the incident, the student worker acted with professionalism, restraint, and discretion.”

Sophia’s Future

Due to her racist outburst, Sophia lost her College Fashionista membership and partnership with the Dillard department store. Furthermore, if found guilty of assault, she faces one to five years in prison. Many people are urging the university to expel her, and her future remains uncertain.

The incident involving Sophia Rosing at the University of Kentucky has received widespread attention. Her use of racial slurs and alleged assault have led to several charges, and her future at the university remains uncertain. While her parents have shown support for her, many people are calling for her expulsion, and she may face severe consequences if found guilty.
